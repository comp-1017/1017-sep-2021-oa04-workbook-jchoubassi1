Choosing the Best Apples for pies

So what is the best apple for apple pie? Well this question can start a fight in any kitchen. SO you want a tart and tangy apple witha firm flesh. Maybe you like a splash of sweetness or maybe a firm fleshy apple is more to your liking. When it comes to choosing your apple for Grandma's apple pie we recommen using Granny Smith, Honeycrisp, or Mutsu

Granny Smith
Grany SMith apples are generally thought of as the go-to baking apple, but in the BA test kitchen, we have a few favs that hold up under heat and balance sweet-tart flavour. Try them when you're ready to branch out.
Honeycrisp
Honeycrisp is our desert-island apple (choosing apples)". It's shatteringly crisp texture is guaranteed to hold firm when baked or caramelized, such as in our Apple-Molasses Upside-down cake or an elegant Tarte tatin.
Mutsu
Mutsu also known as crispin, this firm-fleshed, less tart option is similar in flavour to a Golden Delicious. Great for pies or other recipes that call for gentle cooking.
